Voices Rising

LGBTQ of Color ARTs & Culture

Mission Statement

Voices Rising: LGBTQ of Color Arts & Culture is an arts incubator for queer communities of color. We foreground Black,Indigenous, POC/multicultural arts & cuture, and by documenting our shared stories we create repository for that culture. In 2007, Voices Risinig began as a grassrouts cultural organization at Richard Hugo house. Since then we have presented at Langston Hughes Cultural Center, and we are Anchor Tenants at Washington Hall. We have produced over thirty cutural events since 2007, including interdisciplinary performance, dance, live art, generative workshops, and most recently- a culture bearing quilt created in a series of community quilting circles. We work from an intersectional space, inviting multigenerational wisdom sharing and mentorship. We cultivate health and healing through the arts, for communities that have been deeply impacted by systems of oppression. We have provided nuturance and elevationto many artists of color, including THEESatisfaction, Soulchilde, Dakota, Amber Flame, Justin Huertas and many others. Many have gone on to successful careers in the arts. In the Two Spirit tradition, we work "to keep the fire lit in the village", meaning our work has existed as a means of community empowerment, healing and evolution.. This funding will allow us to grow this project into a sustainable cultural asset for years to come. Ouur mission has always been to be of sercive to our community, and for years this work was volunteer. We welcome your support to continue to serve all of our people.

Vision Statement

We are in a transformational/evolutionary stage at this moment, currenlty invinting a wider, intergenerational community to share what is most needed, and how we may best be of service. Current plans are ongoing for a Hope Corps project, free to all, focused on BIPO/QTIPOC community led gatherings and offerings, of creative workshops/trainig/pportunities,mentorships,healing modalities/self care/mutual and material aid. We've grown from preseting interdisciplinary performance events, to a uniquely intersectional organization that operates from the Two Spirit philosophy: To keep the fire lit in the village. Our recent project at King county steet station was "Home of Good: A Black Seattle Storyquilt", a yearlong installation of a community-created quilt comosed of photographs of Black life in Seattle's Central District 1900-1990s. Collaborations happend with Goethe Institutes Trainspotting Series, and continue with the community artist Free Egynfemi Bangura and Wa Na Wari.
